# Brain-Computer Interfaces for Music

## Musical BCIs

### Brain-Computer Music Interfacing
**Miranda, E. R., & Castet, J. (2014)** - *Guide to Brain-Computer Music Interfacing*
- **Link**: [ISBN: 978-1-4471-6583-2](https://www.academia.edu/118644287/Guide_to_Brain_Computer_Music_Interfacing) | https://jmladeno.net/wp-content/uploads/2017/07/Springer.pdf
- **Summary**: Comprehensive overview of BCIs designed for musical applications, from performance to composition.
- **Relevance**: Foundational reference for musical BCI design principles.

### The BCMI-Piano
**Wu, D., et al. (2010)** - *Online and offline analysis of EEG-based BCI motor imagery using the k-nearest neighbor classifier*
- **Link**: https://doi.org/10.1109/IEMBS.2010.5626782
- **Summary**: Motor imagery BCI for controlling musical parameters in real-time.
- **Relevance**: Demonstrates feasibility of real-time EEG-based music control.

### Affective BCIs for Music
**Williams, D., et al. (2018)** - *Affective calibration of musical feature sets in an emotionally intelligent music composition system*
- **Link**: https://doi.org/10.1145/3243274.3243282
- **Summary**: System that adapts music generation based on user's emotional state detected from physiological signals.
- **Relevance**: Shows how emotion recognition can inform generative music systems.

## EEG-Based Musical Interfaces

### Passive Brain-Computer Interfaces
**Zander, T. O., & Kothe, C. (2011)** - *Towards passive brain-computer interfaces: applying brain-computer interface technology to human-machine interaction in general*
- **Link**: https://doi.org/10.3217/jucs-017-09-1305
- **Summary**: Passive BCIs monitor cognitive states without explicit control commands; suitable for adaptive systems.
- **Relevance**: Philosophy aligns with augmentation rather than replacement paradigm.

### Real-Time Emotion Detection from EEG
**Jenke, R., et al. (2014)** - *Feature extraction and selection for emotion recognition from EEG*
- **Link**: https://doi.org/10.1109/TAFFC.2014.2339834
- **Summary**: Systematic evaluation of EEG features for emotion recognition.
- **Relevance**: Practical methods for extracting affective information from EEG in real-time.

## Auditory BCIs

### Auditory Attention Decoding
**O'Sullivan, J. A., et al. (2015)** - *Attentional Selection in a Cocktail Party Environment Can Be Decoded from Single-Trial EEG*
- **Link**: https://doi.org/10.1093/cercor/bhu044
- **Summary**: EEG can decode which speaker a person is attending to in multi-speaker environments.
- **Relevance**: Demonstrates high temporal resolution auditory decoding from EEG.

### Music Imagery Detection
**Schaefer, R. S., et al. (2011)** - *Auditory rhythmic cueing in movement rehabilitation: findings and possible mechanisms*
- **Link**: https://doi.org/10.1098/rstb.2010.0333
- **Summary**: Neural signatures of imagined music distinct from perceived music.
- **Relevance**: Potential for detecting internal musical imagination.

## Creative BCIs

### Generative Art with Neural Signals
**Nigam, S., et al. (2019)** - *Brain-Computer Interfaces for Creative Expression*
- **Link**: https://doi.org/10.3389/fnins.2019.00559
- **Summary**: Survey of BCIs designed for artistic and creative applications rather than assistive purposes.
- **Relevance**: Aligns with project's focus on creativity enhancement.

### Interactive Sonification
**Hermann, T., et al. (2011)** - *Sonification for Exploratory Data Analysis*
- **Link**: https://doi.org/10.1007/978-1-4471-2458-5
- **Summary**: Principles of mapping data to sound for information communication and exploration.
- **Relevance**: Design principles for brain-to-sound mappings.

## Therapeutic Applications

### Music Therapy with BCIs
**Muller-Putz, G. R., et al. (2015)** - *Towards noninvasive hybrid brain-computer interfaces: framework, practice, clinical application, and beyond*
- **Link**: https://doi.org/10.1109/JPROC.2015.2411333
- **Summary**: Hybrid BCIs combining multiple modalities for improved reliability in therapeutic contexts.
- **Relevance**: Robustness important for well-being applications.

### Neurofeedback and Music
**Gruzelier, J. H. (2014)** - *EEG-neurofeedback for optimising performance*
- **Link**: https://doi.org/10.1016/j.neubiorev.2013.09.015
- **Summary**: Neurofeedback training can enhance cognitive and creative performance.
- **Relevance**: Potential for training users to enhance creative brain states.

## Technical Challenges

### Signal Quality and Artifacts
**Urigüen, J. A., & Garcia-Zapirain, B. (2015)** - *EEG artifact removal—state-of-the-art and guidelines*
- **Link**: https://doi.org/10.1016/j.jneumeth.2015.01.012
- **Summary**: Comprehensive review of EEG artifact removal techniques.
- **Relevance**: Essential for reliable real-time BCI systems.

### User Training and Adaptation
**Lotte, F., et al. (2013)** - *A review of classification algorithms for EEG-based brain-computer interfaces*
- **Link**: https://doi.org/10.1088/1741-2560/4/2/R01
- **Summary**: Machine learning approaches for BCI; importance of co-adaptive systems.
- **Relevance**: Users and systems must adapt together for effective BCIs.

## Design Principles

### User-Centered BCI Design
**Kübler, A., et al. (2014)** - *A user-centred approach for bringing BCI controlled applications to end-users*
- **Link**: https://doi.org/10.1186/1743-0003-11-1
- **Summary**: Emphasizes importance of user needs, expectations, and iterative design.
- **Relevance**: Essential philosophy for creative BCIs that enhance rather than constrain.

### Embodied Interaction with BCIs
**Dourish, P. (2001)** - *Where the Action Is: The Foundations of Embodied Interaction*
- **Link**: ISBN: 0-262-04196-0
- **Summary**: Theoretical framework for understanding interaction as embodied, situated practice.
- **Relevance**: BCIs should support embodied musical experience, not disembodied control.

## Ethical Considerations

### BCI Ethics
**Steinert, S., et al. (2019)** - *Doing the right thing: a qualitative study on user perspectives of brain-computer interface ethics*
- **Link**: https://doi.org/10.1016/j.neuroimage.2019.03.045
- **Summary**: User perspectives on privacy, agency, and identity with BCIs.
- **Relevance**: Directly informs ethical design of brain-music systems.

### Neurorights and Cognitive Liberty
**Ienca, M., & Andorno, R. (2017)** - *Towards new human rights in the age of neuroscience and neurotechnology*
- **Link**: https://doi.org/10.1016/j.lfs.2017.05.021
- **Summary**: Proposes new rights protecting mental privacy and cognitive liberty.
- **Relevance**: Establishes ethical framework for neurotechnology research.

## Key Insights

- Musical BCIs should augment, not replace human creativity
- Passive monitoring more suitable than explicit control for creative contexts
- Real-time processing requires careful feature selection and efficient algorithms
- User training and system adaptation both necessary
- Robustness and reliability crucial for practical systems
- Ethical design must preserve agency, privacy, and authenticity
